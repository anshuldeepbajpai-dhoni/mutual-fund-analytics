# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Findings

## 1. Mutual Fund Industry Growth
The mutual fund industry witnessed strong growth between 2022 and 2025, with total folio counts increasing from 13.26 crore to 26.12 crore, indicating rising investor participation across the country.

## 2. SIP Inflow Trends
Monthly SIP inflows showed a consistent upward trend, crossing major milestones during the analysis period. This reflects growing investor confidence and increasing adoption of systematic investment plans.

## 3. Geographic Distribution of Investments
T30 cities contributed approximately 65.9% of total investment value, while B30 cities accounted for 34.1%, demonstrating the expanding penetration of mutual funds beyond metropolitan regions.

## 4. Sector Allocation Patterns
Banking emerged as the largest sector allocation, followed by IT, Pharma, and Automobile sectors. The portfolio distribution indicates a preference for diversified exposure across major industries.

## 5. Portfolio Concentration Analysis
The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) analysis revealed that certain funds maintain highly concentrated sector allocations, which may increase both return potential and concentration risk.

## 6. Risk and Return Characteristics
Small-cap and mid-cap funds delivered higher annualized returns but also exhibited greater volatility compared to large-cap and debt-oriented schemes, highlighting the risk-return tradeoff.

## 7. Sharpe Ratio Behaviour
Rolling 90-day Sharpe ratios fluctuated significantly across different funds and market cycles, with several equity funds achieving strong risk-adjusted performance during bullish periods.

## 8. Downside Risk Analysis
Historical VaR and CVaR analysis showed that small-cap and mid-cap funds possess higher downside risk, whereas large-cap and debt funds demonstrated relatively lower risk exposure.

## 9. Benchmark Comparison
Top-performing mutual funds consistently outperformed benchmark indices such as NIFTY 50 and NIFTY 100 over the trailing three-year period, indicating effective active fund management.

## 10. Correlation Among Funds
Daily return correlation analysis suggested moderate diversification benefits across different categories, with most funds exhibiting low to moderate correlation coefficients.

## 11. Investor Behaviour Analysis
The average SIP amount increased across investor cohorts, suggesting higher investment commitments from newer investors and growing confidence in long-term wealth creation through mutual funds.

## Conclusion
The exploratory analysis highlights robust growth in India's mutual fund industry, driven by increasing SIP participation, rising folio counts, strong equity market performance, and expanding investor awareness. While higher-return categories such as small-cap and mid-cap funds offer attractive growth opportunities, they also carry elevated risk levels, emphasizing the importance of portfolio diversification and risk management.
